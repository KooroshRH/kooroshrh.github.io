Research

My Ph.D. work is supervised by Prof. Atena Roshan Fekr and Prof. Alex Mihailidis at the University of Toronto. The main thread is machine learning for sensing and perception in clinical environments: 3D point clouds from radar and depth sensors, caregiver activity recognition, and models that connect human motion to questions around infection transmission. I care about data quality, calibration, and latency when methods leave the lab.

I also work on generative modeling, including diffusion-based image generation, building on earlier work on video-to-video translation and style transfer. Outside the university I build applied ML systems in industry, which means staying close to deployment, monitoring, and the gap between research prototypes and production services.
