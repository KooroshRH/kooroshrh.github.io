Research

Most of my days orbit learning problems that look like computer vision and sequence modeling: 3D point clouds from radar and depth stacks, caregiver activity recognition, and the messy coupling between human motion and infection transmission when you try to treat a hospital as a sensor-rich dynamical system rather than a slide deck. The thesis, with Prof. Roshan Fekr and Prof. Alex Mihailidis, keeps that thread honest about data limits and latency.

I also spend time on the generative side of the house — lately diffusion and image synthesis, building on earlier work on video appearance and style transfer — and I still like the feeling of shipping models in the wild. Industry work is part of the same habit: a reminder that papers and production traffic rarely share the same assumptions.

Background — briefly

Education

Ph.D. at the University of Toronto (2023 onward; expected 2028): learning and sensing in clinical environments, with the usual detours through point clouds, sequences, and deployment friction. B.Sc. in Computer Engineering from Amirkabir University of Technology (Tehran Polytechnic), 2023.

Teaching & community

Tutorial teaching assistant in Computer Science at Toronto (systems, microprocessors, software design). Previously head TA at Amirkabir across algorithms, AI, and programming courses. Occasional peer reviewer for Circuits, Systems, and Signal Processing; recipient of the EPIC Doctoral Award (2025).

Beyond the lab

I lead applied AI work at Masterly in Toronto (products spanning document intelligence, video understanding, and LLM-backed financial tooling, mostly on AWS). Earlier roles span computer-vision R&D and shipped game client work — details live in the PDF vitæ if you need the full chronology.
