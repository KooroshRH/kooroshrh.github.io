What I work on

Hospitals are busy, messy, wonderfully human places — and they are also places where small lapses in routine can carry outsized consequences. I am drawn to questions that sit at the boundary between measurement and meaning: what can we responsibly infer about infection exposure when people move, touch surfaces, and interact with equipment, and how should algorithms respect the noise and dignity of real wards?

With Prof. Roshan Fekr and Prof. Alex Mihailidis, my thesis centers on multi-modal sensing (for example mmWave radar, RGB-D, IMU, and BLE) and deep learning for real-time infection-risk estimation and caregiver activity recognition. Alongside the Ph.D., I build applied AI systems in industry — a useful counterweight that keeps me honest about latency, deployment, and what “works” actually means outside a notebook.
